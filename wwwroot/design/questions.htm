Considering signing up? Allow us to address some of the reservations you might have.

Why should I have a technical implementation partner at all?

Well, for one thing, we'll sometimes bring you some business. More importantly, having a relationship with a reliable partner will allow you to move quickly to bid on the bigger projects that require a full-service solution. While some firms can offer both design and implementation services in-house, we've had good success proposing best-of-breed approaches, combining a design specialist with an implementation specialist. If you find yourself needing to make such a bid, we're happy to help with the proposal and the associated sales work, with you keeping the lead on the engagement.

Why not just use the client's internal technical staff to implement our designs?

Sometimes that works fine. More often, though, it doesn't. Most often, internal IT staff have skills oriented towards long-term system maintenance and enhancement. And even those unusual clients with good internal development teams tend to have experience with less visually-oriented back-end systems. They've typically worked rarely with design firms before and have no experience with the kind of careful and creative use of cutting-edge UI implementation techniques that a good design implementation requires. We've done it before many times, and we've had great success in realizing the visions of our design partners, adhering to their designs as closely as possible while meeting requirements for cross-browser compatibility and scalability.

Of course, leaving the work to the client's internal staff also reduces the project's billable hours. When a project brings us significant billable work, we're happy to share some of our revenues with the partner that brought us in.

Why don't I hire my own developers and keep all the work to myself?

Quite a few firms do. It's no small undertaking, though. Hiring and managing great programmers is a difficult art, quite different than managing most other types of professional employees. A great programmer is about 10 times more productive than the average one, and a bad programmer is often worse than having no one at all. We're not experts in design, but we suspect the latter is also true of designers; who wants someone who produces mediocre designs? We've been hiring developers for years, and we know how to pick the great ones. We've built our success on hiring and nurturing only the highest quality technical talent.

Even if you did build a good development team, you'd have to keep them busy and billable. That would change how you develop business and the sorts of projects you pursue. We've found that the great design firms, focusing on bringing in the work at which they excel, produce only sporadic software development work, the kind that is most appropriate for a partner.

Why is Nevo the right partner?

Nevo has made its reputation on the quality of its staff. They are of the greatest talent, and they bring to bear the wisdom of many years of experience working on software projects. Again and again, we have been able to deliver projects on time, on budget, with high quality, and with the kind of attention to detail that your designs require. Our people know how to make a good design vision into reality, and we have the project experience to prove it. We are acutely aware that our survival depends solely on our reputation. Our projects are always successes, because they have to be.

As a more mundane consideration, keep in mind that Nevo believes that good visual design is a core competency that doesn't fit in its technically-oriented culture. Your work will always complement ours, not compete with it.
